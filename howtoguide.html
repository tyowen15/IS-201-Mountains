The Ultimate Guide to Surviving a Night in the Mountains

Step-by-Step Survival Guide

Each step includes an image and guidance on how to perform it effectively while stranded in mountain terrain.

1. Assess injuries & first aid.
Stop bleeding, stabilize injuries, and treat early hypothermia.
2. Create or find shelter.
Use tents, tarps, rocks, or trees to block wind and preserve heat.
3. Stay dry & insulate.
Swap wet layers for dry ones and add insulation to conserve heat.
4. Build a heat source.
Make a safe fire if possible. Never build fires inside enclosed shelters.
5. Eat warm, high-energy foods.
Your body burns calories fast in the cold—fuel it.
6. Signal for rescue.
Use whistles, fires, mirrors, or flashlight signals (3 flashes = distress).
7. Conserve energy.
Move only when necessary. Staying put increases rescue chances.
